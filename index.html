Human Function Learning in Varying Modalities

My masters' research project was concerned with a cognitive science question: the exploration of the effect that data availability (memory) has on human function learning and the resulting extrapolation patterns. It involved the development of a Javascript-based application and its deployment as as an experimental tool to collect participant responses. The collected data was then analysed and we found that a simple memory-limited Bayesian model is consistent with human extrapolations for linear data.

CodeYourFuture

CodeYourFuture is a volunteer-run coding school and community that teaches refugees and asylum seekers web development. I help with the organisation, lesson design and mentoring of the students. My main topics are introductory Javascript, code design, usability and UX. I also organise taster classes as part of our work. Examining the effect of copyright in games

The Center for Copyright Research of the University of Glasgow organised a hackathon centered around law and copyright themes. My team and I won the competition by constructing an automated system of gathering and examining evidence of how copyright protection (high or low) can impact creative production in the video game industry, using text mining and data analysis on a large corpus of creative goods found on online marketplaces.
